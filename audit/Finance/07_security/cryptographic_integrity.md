# Security: Cryptographic Integrity

## 1. Immutable Audit Chaining
The system employs a dual-chain strategy to prevent "After-the-fact" database tampering.

### A. Journal Entry Chain
- **Algorithm**: `SHA-256`.
- **Payload**: `lastHash | tenantId | companyId | journalId | timestamp | sortedLines`.
- **Storage**: `JournalEntry.entryHash`.
- **Verification**: `LedgerInvariantService.validatePreviousHashLink` (Hot Path).

### B. Audit Log Chain
- **Algorithm**: `SHA-256`.
- **Payload**: `tenantId | userId | action | entityId | correlationId | previousHash`.
- **Storage**: `AuditLog.hashChain`.
- **Verification**: `AuditChainService.repairChain` (Admin Path).

## 2. Certified Reporting
- **Snapshots**: Every fiscal period close generates a Merkle Root of all journals in that period.
- **Anchoring**: The root is stored in `LedgerHashAnchor`. This anchor can be published to a public chain or a private auditor's vault to prove the state of the books was not altered after the close.

## 3. PDF/Export Integrity
- Reports exported via the dashboard are signed with an HMAC `signature`.
- The `/verify-export` endpoint allows recipients to verify the file was generated by Zenvix and hasn't been edited.

## 🔥 Locked Procurement Workflow (Enterprise Standard)

### Phase 1 — Department Request

1. Employee submits **Requisition Request**
2. Request must be approved by:

#### ✅ Requester HOD Approval (Mandatory)

Only after approval does it enter Procurement pipeline.

---

### Phase 2 — Procurement Draft & Categorization

Procurement team:

- Collects approved requisitions
- Categorizes by:

- Supplier
- Product type
- Branch location
- Contract type
- Budget class

Procurement creates:

- Draft Purchase Order
- Draft Contract Request (if required)

---

### Phase 3 — Procurement HOD Approval (Draft Gate)

Before supplier engagement:

#### ✅ Procurement HOD must approve the draft

This prevents unauthorized supplier commitments.

---

### Phase 4 — Supplier Price Verification

Once approved, procurement sends supplier:

- Draft PO
- Product list
- Requested pricing confirmation

This is done via:

- Email (PDF attachment)
- Supplier Portal (preferred)

Supplier must:

- Confirm pricing
- Propose changes
- Upload quotation proof

---

### Phase 5 — Legal Contract Collaboration (Mandatory)

Contracts are **not owned by Procurement**.

#### Legal Department Responsibilities

- Draft contract templates
- Review supplier terms
- Ensure compliance
- Maintain contract version control

#### Contract Signing Requirements

Contracts must be signed by:

- Supplier
- Procurement HOD
- Finance HOD
- Legal approval recorded

---

### Phase 6 — Final Multi-HOD Approval (PO Release Gate)

After supplier confirmation:

Final approval requires:

- ✅ Procurement HOD
- ✅ Requester HOD
- ✅ Finance HOD

Only after all approvals:

#### Purchase Order can be released

---

### Phase 7 — PO Release + Execution

PO is issued and distributed:

- Supplier receives PO
- Inventory receives expected delivery notice
- Finance receives payable commitment
- Rating engine begins monitoring supplier performance
